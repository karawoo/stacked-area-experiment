Training Task: Instructions

Please look at each of the charts below and determine which segment of the chart is smaller at the point where the chart is marked. Then, provide a quick visual judgment of what percentage the smaller segment is of the larger segment. Only consider the sizes of each segment at the point where the chart is marked with the letters A and B. You can imagine drawing a vertical line through this point and comparing the segments to determine which is longer. However, do not try to make precise measurements, either mentally or with a physical object such as a pencil or your finger. We are interested in how well people make these judgments spontaneously and without the help of external aids.

This is a training task that will illustrate the types of questions we are asking. In the main task, the charts will not be marked with lines and question 2 will have a write-in box instead of multiple choice answers. If you accept additional HITs in this batch, you will not need to repeat the training task.
